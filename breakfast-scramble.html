4.2 Breakfast scramble

This is a favorite of mine (though I like the huevos rancheros better). Very easy, filling, and delicious. Plenty of room for variation as well.

Source: Imitating a fairly common restaurant dish.

Ingredients:

Ingredients

Tools

2-3 medium red potatoes

Frying pan/skillet x2

1/8 lb breakfast sausage

Spatulas

1-2 Eggs

Onion

Green pepper

Cooking oil

Preparation:

  1. Set your skillet (if you have one, frying pan if not) to heating. I tend to do this at medium to medium/high heat.
  2. Dice up the potatoes. I usually make them into chunks about 1/4-1/2 inch. No need to peel them, just wash and cut off any roots that may have started growing.
  3. Throw some high-heat oil (such as avocado oil) or bacon grease into the skillet (if you have one, regular frying pan if not), and the dump in the potatoes. Be careful of oil/grease splattering. Stir the potatoes around to get them all coated with the oil, and then spread them as evenly as you can.
  4. In another pan, start frying the sausage.
  5. Occassionally stir the potatoes around so that they get crisped on all sides (this is the reason for the medium/high heat).
  6. Sautee a handful of diced onion and green peppers in a seperate pan (could be the one you used for sausage).
  7. Once the sausage is done, you can add it to the potatoes.
  8. Once your potatoes are almost done, start frying your egg(s) however you like. Scrambled works great, though I prefer over-medium.
  9. Mix up the potatoes, sausage, and onion/green peppers. Add the eggs on top, salt and pepper to taste.

Variations:

  • Drizzle some BBQ sauce over the top. I rather like BBQ Carnivores sauces for this.
  • Cheese is a good addition to this dish.
  • Instead of breakfast sausage, you can use bacon (though I don’t think it works quite as well), pulled pork, or brisket.

Notes:

  • A slice of toast with butter complements this meal very nicely.
  • You can put the onion and green pepper in with the potatoes, but then the potatoes tend to absorb the moisture and lose their crispyness, which I don’t like.
